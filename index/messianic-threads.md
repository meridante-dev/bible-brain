# The Messianic threads — Bible-Brain

The authored interpretive layer: the Tanakh and the New Testament read as one
story. Each thread is the community's confession, graded by how the New Testament
itself grounds it, and always distinct from the corpus text beneath it (see
[CONSTITUTION.md](../CONSTITUTION.md)). Refs link Tanakh anchor → NT fulfillment.


## Messianic prophecy — the NT declares these fulfilled

### Thirty pieces of silver, the potter's field
*The price of the betrayal, cast to the potter.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Zechariah 11:12-13 → **NT:** Matthew 27:9-10  
**Basis:** Matthew 27:9-10 cites the thirty pieces and the potter's field.  

### The ruler from Bethlehem
*The eternal ruler comes from the least of Judah's clans.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Micah 5:2 → **NT:** Matthew 2:5-6  
**Basis:** Matthew 2:5-6 quotes Micah 5:2 as the chief priests' answer to Herod.  

### Light dawns on Galilee of the nations
*The great light to a people in darkness.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Isaiah 9:1-2 → **NT:** Matthew 4:14-16  
**Basis:** Matthew 4:14-16 quotes Isaiah 9:1-2 of Messiah's Galilean ministry.  

### A new covenant, the law written on the heart
*The promised covenant, inaugurated in the cup.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Jeremiah 31:31-34 → **NT:** Hebrews 8:8-12, Luke 22:20  
**Basis:** Hebrews 8:8-12 quotes Jeremiah 31 in full; Luke 22:20 names the new covenant.  
**Key words:** בְּרִית (be.rit, H1285, “covenant”) · διαθήκη (diathēkē, G1242, “covenant”)

### Not one of his bones broken
*The Passover-lamb rule applied to the crucified Messiah.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Exodus 12:46, Psalms 34:20 → **NT:** John 19:33-36  
**Basis:** John 19:36 cites 'not one of his bones will be broken' (Passover lamb / Ps 34:20).  

### Out of Egypt I called my son
*Corporate Israel and the Messiah share one calling out of Egypt.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Hosea 11:1 → **NT:** Matthew 2:15  
**Basis:** Matthew 2:15 quotes Hosea 11:1; Israel's exodus recapitulated in Messiah.  

### They pierced my hands and feet / cast lots for my clothing
*The psalm of the forsaken one, read at the cross.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Psalms 22:16, Psalms 22:18 → **NT:** John 19:23-24, John 19:37  
**Basis:** John 19:24 quotes Psalm 22:18 (lots for the garment); crucifixion imagery of Ps 22.  

### A priest forever after the order of Melchizedek
*A king-priest whose order predates and outlasts Levi.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Psalms 110:1, Psalms 110:4 → **NT:** Hebrews 5:6, Hebrews 7:17, Matthew 22:44  
**Basis:** Hebrews 5-7 quotes Psalm 110:4; Jesus cites Psalm 110:1 (Matthew 22:44).  
**Key words:** כֹּהֵן (ko.hen, H3548, “priest”) · ἱερεύς (hiereus, G2409, “priest”)

### The stone the builders rejected
*Rejected by builders, made the head of the corner.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Psalms 118:22-23 → **NT:** 1 Peter 2:7, Acts 4:11, Matthew 21:42  
**Basis:** Jesus (Matthew 21:42) and Peter (Acts 4:11) quote Psalm 118:22 of the rejection.  

### You will not abandon me to Sheol
*The holy one not left to see corruption.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Psalms 16:10 → **NT:** Acts 13:35-37, Acts 2:25-32  
**Basis:** Peter (Acts 2:25-31) and Paul (Acts 13:35) read Psalm 16:10 as resurrection.  

### I will pour out my Spirit on all flesh
*Prophecy, dreams, and deliverance for all who call on the Name.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Joel 3:1-5 → **NT:** Acts 2:16-21  
**Basis:** Peter at Pentecost (Acts 2:16-21) quotes Joel 2:28-32 as fulfilled.  
**Key words:** πνεῦμα (pneuma, G4151, “spirit/breath: spirit”)

### The suffering servant, pierced for our transgressions
*The central Servant Song, the atonement's Tanakh spine.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Isaiah 52:13-15, Isaiah 53:1-12 → **NT:** 1 Peter 2:22-25, Acts 8:32-35, Matthew 8:17  
**Basis:** Acts 8:32-35 reads Isaiah 53 of Messiah; 1 Peter 2 & Matthew 8:17 apply it.  

### Your king comes, humble, on a donkey
*The gentle king, not a war-horse.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Zechariah 9:9 → **NT:** John 12:14-15, Matthew 21:4-5  
**Basis:** Matthew 21:4-5 and John 12:14-15 both quote Zechariah 9:9 at the entry.  

### Born of a virgin, 'God with us'
*The sign of Immanuel read as the incarnation.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Isaiah 7:14 → **NT:** Matthew 1:22-23  
**Basis:** Matthew 1:22-23 quotes Isaiah 7:14 and names its fulfillment ('Immanuel').  
**Key words:** עַלְמָה (al.mah, H5959, “maiden”) · παρθένος (parthenos, G3933, “virgin”)

### A voice preparing the way
*The forerunner who makes the way straight.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Isaiah 40:3, Malachi 3:1 → **NT:** Mark 1:2-3, Matthew 3:3  
**Basis:** Mark 1:2-3 conflates Malachi 3:1 and Isaiah 40:3 for John the Baptist.  


## Type & foreshadow — the shadow and its substance

### The binding of Isaac, the only son
*The father, the only son, the wood, the provided lamb — offered as type.*  
**Confidence — graded** (a classical typological reading, offered as such)  
**Tanakh:** Genesis 22:1-14 → **NT:** Hebrews 11:17-19, John 3:16, Romans 8:32  
**Basis:** Classical reading; Romans 8:32 echoes 'did not spare his own Son' (cf. Gen 22:12).  

### Adam → the last Adam
*One man's fall answered by one man's obedience.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Genesis 2:7, Genesis 3:17-19 → **NT:** 1 Corinthians 15:22, 1 Corinthians 15:45, Romans 5:14-19  
**Basis:** Romans 5 and 1 Corinthians 15:45 name Christ the 'last Adam' explicitly.  

### The bronze serpent lifted up
*Look and live — the lifted-up Son.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Numbers 21:8-9 → **NT:** John 3:14-15  
**Basis:** John 3:14-15 makes the type explicit: 'as Moses lifted up the serpent...'  
**Key words:** נָחָשׁ (na.chash, H5175, “serpent”) · ὄφις (ophis, G3789, “snake”)

### Jonah three days in the deep
*The sign given to a demanding generation.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Jonah 2:1 → **NT:** Matthew 12:39-40  
**Basis:** Matthew 12:40 names 'the sign of Jonah' for the three days in the tomb.  

### The Passover lamb → the Lamb of God
*The blood on the doorpost read as the atonement's shadow.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** Exodus 12:1-13 → **NT:** 1 Corinthians 5:7, 1 Peter 1:19, John 1:29  
**Basis:** 1 Corinthians 5:7 states 'Christ our Passover is sacrificed'; John 1:29 the Lamb.  
**Key words:** שֶׂה (seh, H7716, “sheep”) · ἀμνός (amnos, G0286, “lamb”)

### Melchizedek, king-priest of Salem
*Bread and wine, a priesthood without genealogy.*  
**Confidence — medium** (clear NT allusion / near-universal reading)  
**Tanakh:** Genesis 14:18-20 → **NT:** Hebrews 7:1-10  
**Basis:** Hebrews 7 develops Genesis 14 into the Melchizedek priesthood argument.  


## Thematic threads — one promise across the whole canon

### The seed of the woman who crushes the serpent
*The canon's first promise, traced to its last book.*  
**Confidence — graded** (a classical typological reading, offered as such)  
**Tanakh:** Genesis 3:15 → **NT:** Galatians 4:4, Revelation 12:5, Romans 16:20  
**Basis:** The protoevangelium read forward; Romans 16:20 echoes crushing the serpent.  
**Key words:** זֶ֫רַע (ze.ra, H2233, “seed: children”)

### The everlasting throne of David
*The Davidic covenant carried to its eternal king.*  
**Confidence — high** (the NT itself cites this text)  
**Tanakh:** II Samuel 7:12-16, Isaiah 9:6-7 → **NT:** Acts 2:29-36, Luke 1:32-33  
**Basis:** Luke 1:32-33 promises the throne of David; Acts 2 argues its fulfillment.  

### The Lamb, from Passover to the throne
*One image binding the whole canon: the Lamb slain and enthroned.*  
**Confidence — medium** (clear NT allusion / near-universal reading)  
**Tanakh:** Exodus 12:5-6, Isaiah 53:7 → **NT:** John 1:29, Revelation 5:6-12  
**Basis:** The Lamb motif spans Exodus, Isaiah 53:7 (led like a lamb), John 1:29, Revelation 5.
